What is a band gap?


THEREFORE...

Band gap is the energy difference between the top of the valence band and the bottom of the conduction band in a material. In insulators and semiconductors, electrons need a certain minimum energy (band gap energy) to move from the valence band to the conduction band and become mobile carriers of electrical current.
If this band gap is large, the material is an insulator because electrons can't easily jump across it. If the band gap is smaller, the material is a semiconductor because electrons can jump across and conduct electricity under certain conditions.
